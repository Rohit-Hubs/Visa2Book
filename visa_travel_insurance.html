Common Questions

Everything you need to know about visas and insurance

How long does visa processing usually take?

Processing times vary significantly depending on the destination country, visa type, and current workload at the embassy/consulate. Generally:

  • Tourist visas: 5-15 working days
  • Business visas: 1-3 weeks
  • Student visas: 4-8 weeks
  • Work visas: 6-12 weeks

We provide accurate processing time estimates when you start your application and offer expedited services for urgent travel when available.

What documents do I need for a visa application?

While requirements vary by country, most visa applications require:

  • Passport valid for at least 6 months beyond your stay
  • Completed application forms (we help fill these out)
  • Passport-sized photographs meeting specific requirements
  • Proof of financial means (bank statements, sponsorship letters)
  • Travel itinerary including flight and accommodation details
  • Purpose-specific documents:
    • Tourist: invitation letters if visiting friends/family
    • Business: company letter, invitation from host company
    • Student: acceptance letter, proof of tuition payment

Our visa specialists provide a customized checklist for your specific application.

When should I purchase travel insurance?

The ideal time to purchase travel insurance is immediately after booking your trip. This ensures you're covered for:

  • Trip cancellation protection from the moment of purchase
  • Pre-existing condition coverage (if purchased within 14-21 days of initial trip payment)
  • Financial default protection if your travel provider goes bankrupt

However, you can purchase most plans up until the day before departure. Medical coverage only begins when your trip starts.

Does travel insurance cover adventure activities?

Our standard plans cover many recreational activities, but certain adventure sports require additional coverage:

Automatically covered activities: Hiking, snorkeling, cycling, kayaking (up to certain altitudes/depths)

Require adventure sports rider: Scuba diving, skiing/snowboarding, rock climbing, bungee jumping, parasailing

Typically excluded: Professional sports, mountaineering above 6,000m, cave diving

We can add adventure sports coverage to your policy for an additional premium. Be sure to disclose all planned activities when purchasing.
